The Standard

"Excellence isn't a goal. It's the standard."

My background is rooted in the intersection of physical discipline and technical precision. From the rowing shells and courts of Shawnigan Lake School to a formative exchange in France, I learned early on that the most meaningful work happens in the margins between structure and creativity.

002

The Evolution

I chose Mechanical Engineering at the University of Waterloo because it is rigorous. I thrive where the constraints are tightest, whether that is refining a complex surface in CAD or navigating the logic of a new robotic system. I am interested in high-performance environments where technical mastery is the only currency.

003

The Intent

I am currently an engineer in formation, building towards excellence. My work is defined by a commitment to technical logic and a strong interior life. I am here to build things that matter with intention, restraint, and a focus on what is next.
