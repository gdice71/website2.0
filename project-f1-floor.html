ENGINEERING

F1 Floor CAD Design
and CFD Analysis

Self-directed engineering project exploring the impact of the 2026 FIA Formula 1 regulations on ground-effect porpoising. Using SimScale CFD simulations (steady RANS at 65 m/s) and Python-based reduced-order modeling, I compared ride-height sensitivity of downforce and pitch moment between aggressive 2023-style floors and the flatter 2026 design.

Results show ~5.7× lower aerodynamic stiffness sensitivity in 2026 (dFz/dz from −86 kN/m to +15 kN/m), leading to significantly improved damping and reduced oscillation after perturbations. Ongoing work includes transient CFD and full-vehicle coupling.

Built to deepen skills in CFD post-processing, nonlinear fitting, and vehicle dynamics analysis.
